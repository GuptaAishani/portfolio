About

Background & Education

I'm an aerospace engineer focused on the intersection of propulsion system design and flight mechanics. My work spans early-stage conceptual design through test campaign execution, with particular depth in liquid bipropellant engine development and structural analysis of launch vehicle components.

Currently pursuing a PhD at MIT AeroAstro investigating turbulent combustion instabilities in LOX/methane engines, with experimental work conducted at the MIT Gas Turbine Laboratory and in collaboration with the SpaceX Raptor team.

Previously I contributed to trajectory analysis and GNC development for a 3U CubeSat constellation at NASA JPL, and supported structural qualification of a commercial small launch vehicle's first-stage thrust frame at Rocket Lab.

Finite element analysis of a composite overwrap thrust frame for a small launch vehicle. Defined load cases from flight environments, executed structural test program, correlated FE model to strain gauge data within 4% error.

GNC architecture for a 3U CubeSat constellation in 550km SSO. Designed extended Kalman filter for GPS/IMU fusion and implemented quaternion-feedback pointing and B-dot detumbling algorithms.

04 / AERODYNAMICS
Transonic Nozzle Contour Optimisation
Multi-objective optimisation of rocket nozzle contours minimising separation risk and maximising vacuum Isp. Coupled adjoint CFD with gradient-based optimiser achieving 2.3% Isp improvement over baseline.

Launch Vehicle Mission Architecture Trade Study
End-to-end architecture study for a smallsat rideshare LV targeting 250–500kg to SSO. Assessed propellant selection, staging strategy, fairing sizing, and trajectory optimisation using parametric mass models and GMAT simulation.
