About

Background & Education

I'm a junior at the University of Texas at Austin studying Aerospace Engineering with a Materials Science minor. My core engineering interests sit at the intersection of structural analysis, CAD design, and systems integration — the work of making complex hardware actually come together and fly.

Currently I'm assembling spacecraft at Firefly Aerospace under NASA's CLPS program — soldering harnesses, integrating avionics panels, and supporting vibration qualification testing in a cleanroom environment. Simultaneously, I lead the Space Systems Integration team at Texas Space Society, designing modular orbital and lunar habitat architectures.

My long-term mission is permanent lunar residency. I believe the structural and integration challenges of building habitats that can sustain tens of thousands of people on the Moon are the most important engineering problems of our generation, and I intend to spend my career solving them.
